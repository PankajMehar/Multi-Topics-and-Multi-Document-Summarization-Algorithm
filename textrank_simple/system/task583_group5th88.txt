Prices of computers and chocolate are likely to rise in early signs of inflation spurred by the falling value of the pound since Britain voted to leave the EU.
Other computer makers are likely to increase UK prices following the pound’s fall to 31-year lows this week – a drop of more than 10% since the referendum.
Smartphone maker OnePlus said it would raise the price of its OnePlus 3 model from £309 to £329 from 11 July, also attributing the hike to the post-Brexit vote fall in the pound.
The remain campaign predicted higher prices if Britain voted to leave the EU due to the higher cost of imports from the pound’s likely fall.
Economists are predicting further falls in sterling’s value as the economy slows and some have raised the prospect of the pound slumping to parity with the US dollar.Theresa May’s new administration has received a significant boost from a Bank of England report showing that the economy has been resilient in the first few weeks since the Brexit vote and displays no general signs of slowing down.
